The Magical World of Glowing Creatures: Nature's Light Show

By: Harshit Sharma

Imagine walking through a dark forest at night. Suddenly, you see tiny lights flickering around you, like stars that have fallen to the ground. This isn't magic—it's a real phenomenon called bioluminescence, where living creatures produce light.

What is Bioluminescence?

Bioluminescence is when living organisms make and emit light. This light is created through a chemical reaction inside special cells. Two main ingredients make this happen: luciferin (a molecule that produces light) and luciferase (an enzyme that helps the reaction). When luciferin mixes with oxygen, it glows. The color of the light can change depending on the creature and the chemicals involved.
